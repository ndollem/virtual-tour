Economy Grows 5.2% in Q4, Beats Forecasts

Indonesia's economy expanded 5.2% year-on-year in the fourth quarter, exceeding analyst expectations on strong consumption and investment.

Indonesia's gross domestic product grew 5.2% in the fourth quarter compared with a year earlier, the statistics bureau said on Monday, above the 5.0% median forecast in a Reuters poll and the 5.0% growth recorded in the third quarter. On a quarter-on-quarter basis, the economy grew 0.9% in the October–December period, also beating expectations.

Household consumption and fixed capital formation drove the expansion, while government spending and exports provided additional support. Bank Indonesia's recent rate cuts have started to feed through into credit growth, officials said.

"The growth figure confirms that domestic demand remains resilient and that policy support is having an effect," said Dendi Ramdani, chief economist at Bank Mandiri. "We expect full-year growth to land around 5.1% and see room for one more rate cut in 2025 if inflation stays benign."

Manufacturing and services led sectoral growth, while agriculture benefited from favourable weather. The bureau noted that investment in machinery and infrastructure had picked up in the second half of the year.

Finance Minister Indrawati said the government would maintain fiscal discipline while prioritising spending on infrastructure and human capital. "We are on track to meet our deficit target and growth assumptions for the year," she told reporters after the release.

Analysts expect GDP growth to remain in the 5.0–5.3% range in 2025, with risks tilted to the upside if global demand holds and commodity prices stay supportive. The rupiah was little changed after the data release, with traders already pricing in a solid outcome.
