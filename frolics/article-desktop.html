Bank Indonesia Holds Key Rate at 5.75%

Central bank keeps benchmark rate unchanged as inflation stays within target band; governor signals data-dependent stance ahead.

Bank Indonesia left its benchmark seven-day reverse repurchase rate at 5.75% on Thursday, as widely expected, after inflation eased in February and the rupiah stabilised. The decision was unanimous, the central bank said in a statement.

Governor Warjiyo said the current level was "consistent with efforts to bring inflation back to the 1.5%–3.5% target range in the second half of 2025" and to support rupiah stability. He added that the board would remain "data-dependent" and focus on global spillovers and domestic growth.

Twelve of 13 analysts in a Reuters poll had forecast no change. One had expected a 25-basis-point cut. February inflation came in at 2.8% year-on-year, within the target corridor, while the rupiah has traded in a narrow band against the dollar in recent weeks.

Economists expect BI to start cutting rates in the second quarter if inflation remains benign and the Fed begins its easing cycle. "Today's hold was a given; the focus now is on the forward guidance and any hint of a cut timeline," said Enrico Tanuwidjaja of Maybank Indonesia.

BI also held the deposit facility and lending facility rates at 5.00% and 6.50% respectively. The next rate decision is scheduled for April 24.
